My Favourite People

Honorable Prime Minister Mr.Narendra Damodardas Modi

Narendra Modi is the 14th and current Prime Minister of India, having assumed office on May 26, 2014. Born on September 17, 1950, in Vadnagar, Gujarat, Modi rose from humble beginnings, helping his father sell tea at a railway station, to become one of the most influential political leaders in the world. He is a member of the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) and has also served as the Chief Minister of Gujarat from 2001 to 2014. Modi is known for his strong leadership style, strategic decision-making, and focus on economic reforms. Under his leadership, major initiatives like “Make in India,” “Digital India,” “Swachh Bharat Abhiyan,” and “Jan Dhan Yojana” were launched to modernize infrastructure, improve sanitation, promote financial inclusion, and boost manufacturing. His government has placed significant emphasis on technology, digital governance, and self-reliance.Internationally, Modi has worked to strengthen India’s diplomatic relationships with global powers, while asserting India’s role on the world stage. His foreign visits and personal rapport with other world leaders have contributed to enhancing India’s image globally.However, his tenure has also seen controversies and criticisms, particularly regarding issues related to religious tensions, the economy, and democratic freedoms. Decisions such as demonetization in 2016, the implementation of the Goods and Services Tax (GST), and the abrogation of Article 370 in Jammu and Kashmir were seen by many as bold but also highly debated.Despite challenges, Modi remains a highly popular figure with a strong support base. His ability to connect with the masses, use of social media, and portrayal as a leader of the people have made him a central figure in contemporary Indian politics. As of 2025, he continues to shape India's future direction through his vision of a “New India.”

Swatantraveer Vinayak Damodar Savarkar

Vinayak Damodar Savarkar, popularly known as Swatantryaveer Savarkar, was a prominent freedom fighter, poet, writer, and political thinker who played a significant role in India's struggle for independence. Born on May 28, 1883, in Bhagur, Maharashtra, Savarkar was deeply inspired by revolutionary ideas from a young age. He was a brilliant student and an ardent nationalist who believed in armed resistance against British rule. While studying in London, Savarkar became involved with revolutionary groups and authored the book The First War of Indian Independence 1857, which challenged British narratives and portrayed the 1857 revolt as a nationalist movement. His writings and speeches sparked a sense of patriotism among Indians living abroad. In 1909, Savarkar was arrested by the British for his involvement in revolutionary activities. He was sentenced to two life terms of imprisonment totaling fifty years and was transported to the Cellular Jail in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, also known as "Kala Pani." Despite the inhuman treatment and isolation, Savarkar continued to write poems, essays, and letters, keeping the flame of resistance alive. After his release, Savarkar became a leading figure in the Hindu Mahasabha and advocated the philosophy of Hindutva—a concept of cultural nationalism. He was a strong proponent of social reforms such as the abolition of untouchability and caste-based discrimination. Though controversial in some aspects, Savarkar's contribution to India's freedom movement and his emphasis on nationalism and unity remain significant. He passed away on February 26, 1966. Today, he is remembered as a fearless revolutionary and a tireless advocate for India’s sovereignty, earning him the title "Swatantryaveer" or "Brave Freedom Fighter." His life and legacy continue to inspire generations across India.

Rajsaheb Thackeray

Raj Thackeray is a prominent Indian politician and the founder of the Maharashtra Navnirman Sena (MNS), a regional political party based in Maharashtra. Born on June 14, 1968, in Mumbai, he is the nephew of Bal Thackeray, the founder of Shiv Sena. Raj was initially a key member of Shiv Sena and was seen as a potential successor to Bal Thackeray. However, due to internal disagreements and leadership disputes within the party, Raj broke away and formed MNS in 2006. Known for his fiery speeches and strong views on regional identity, Raj Thackeray has often advocated for the rights of Marathi-speaking people in Maharashtra. One of his most controversial and widely discussed stances has been against the influx of North Indian migrants, especially from Uttar Pradesh and Bihar, into Mumbai. His campaigns have drawn both support and criticism — support from those who feel Marathi culture is under threat, and criticism for allegedly promoting divisive politics. Despite his strong rhetoric, Raj Thackeray is also known for his appreciation of art, music, and especially cartoons, much like his uncle Bal Thackeray. He is seen as a charismatic orator and has a significant youth following. Over the years, MNS has seen fluctuating political success. Though it made an impressive debut in the 2009 Maharashtra Assembly elections, its influence has declined in recent years due to changing political dynamics and competition from other regional and national parties. Raj Thackeray remains a dynamic and influential figure in Maharashtra politics. In recent years, he has attempted to rebrand his image and expand MNS's political base by addressing broader issues such as corruption, development, and governance, while still holding on to his core identity politics. His future in politics continues to draw attention across the state.

Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda was a great spiritual leader, philosopher, and patriot of India, born on January 12, 1863, in Kolkata (then Calcutta). His original name was Narendranath Datta. From a young age, he showed deep interest in spirituality, philosophy, and the teachings of Indian scriptures like the Vedas and Upanishads. He was a brilliant student and had a sharp intellect. He became a disciple of the famous saint Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, who greatly influenced his spiritual journey. After the death of his guru, Vivekananda took a vow of renunciation and traveled across India to understand the conditions of the people. He saw poverty, ignorance, and suffering, which deeply moved him and inspired his mission to serve humanity. Swami Vivekananda gained international fame when he represented India at the World’s Parliament of Religions in Chicago in 1893. His opening words, “Sisters and brothers of America,” received a standing ovation from the audience. He spoke about the rich spiritual heritage of India and emphasized the importance of religious tolerance, unity, and the universality of all religions. He founded the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897, which focuses on spiritual teachings, education, health care, and relief work. He believed in the power of youth and urged them to have strength, faith, and self-confidence. His famous quote, “Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached,” continues to inspire generations. Swami Vivekananda passed away on July 4, 1902, at the young age of 39, but his ideas and vision continue to live on. He is remembered as a key figure in the revival of Hinduism in modern India and a messenger of Indian philosophy to the Western world. His teachings emphasize character-building, service to others, and realization of the divine in every being.

Bal Gangadhar Tilak

Bal Gangadhar Tilak, born on July 23, 1856, in Ratnagiri, Maharashtra, was a prominent freedom fighter, nationalist, and social reformer who played a vital role in India's struggle for independence. Known as Lokmanya Tilak, meaning "accepted by the people," he was one of the first leaders to demand full independence (Swaraj) from British rule, declaring, “Swaraj is my birthright, and I shall have it.” Tilak was a scholar of Sanskrit, mathematics, and law, and he used his intellect to inspire people towards nationalism. He believed in reviving Indian culture and history to instill pride among Indians. Through his newspapers Kesari (in Marathi) and The Mahratta (in English), he spread awareness and criticized British policies, often facing imprisonment for his bold writings. Tilak was also a strong advocate for education and co-founded the Deccan Education Society in 1884 to promote nationalistic education. He played a major role in transforming the freedom movement into a mass movement by connecting with people through religious and cultural festivals like Ganesh Utsav and Shivaji Jayanti, using them as platforms to spread patriotic messages. Politically, Tilak was a leader of the extremist faction of the Indian National Congress, advocating for assertive methods like boycotts and swadeshi movements to oppose British rule. His ideas influenced later leaders like Mahatma Gandhi and Subhas Chandra Bose. Despite facing opposition from both the British and moderate Indian leaders, Tilak remained steadfast in his mission. He passed away on August 1, 1920, but his fearless leadership, vision of self-rule, and deep love for the country continue to inspire generations of Indians. Bal Gangadhar Tilak is rightfully remembered as the "Father of Indian Unrest" and a pioneer in India’s journey to freedom.
